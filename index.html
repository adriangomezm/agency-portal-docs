1. Product Overview

What is the Agency Portal?

The Talkpush Agency Portal is a web application that enables organizations to manage their relationships with external staffing/recruitment agencies. Each company has its own separate workspace. It provides a centralized platform where:

  • Staffing agencies submit candidate referrals with resumes
  • Internal recruitment teams track, review, and manage all agency-submitted candidates
  • AI automatically processes resumes and detects duplicate candidates
  • All data syncs bidirectionally with the Talkpush CRM in real time

Who is it for?

AudienceWhat They Do
Talkpush OwnersFull system administrators who manage the entire platform across multiple companies
Company AdminsInternal recruitment managers at a specific company who oversee all agencies, campaigns, and candidates
Agency AdminsAgency managers who manage their team of recruiters and view all their agency's candidates
Agency UsersIndividual agency recruiters who submit and track their own candidates

Key Capabilities

Candidate Management

  • Candidate referral submission with resume upload
  • AI-powered resume parsing & summarization
  • Intelligent duplicate detection with configurable ownership windows
  • Candidate withdrawal workflow

Integration & Analytics

  • Real-time synchronization with Talkpush CRM
  • Multi-country, multi-company support
  • Role-based access control (4-tier hierarchy)
  • Analytics dashboard & Excel export

2. How the Portal Connects to Talkpush

How It All Connects

The Agency Portal is a web application that connects to several external services to power its features:

  • Talkpush CRM — One connection per country. All candidate submissions, resumes, comments, and withdrawals are synced to the CRM in real time.
  • AI Resume Analysis — Uploaded resumes are automatically analyzed by an AI model to generate professional, education, and experience summaries.
  • Email Notifications — Account activation emails, password resets, and invitation reminders are sent automatically.

Data Sync with Talkpush CRM

The Agency Portal connects to one or more Talkpush CRM instances (one per country). Data flows in both directions:

Role Descriptions

Talkpush Owner — Level 1

  • Full system access across all companies
  • Can select a company context to scope their view
  • Can create and manage all entities
  • Only role that can manage companies and import data
  • Some Talkpush Owners have elevated access that allows unrestricted access to all portal features

Company Admin — Level 4

  • Manages everything within their assigned company
  • Can create agencies, campaigns, users
  • Can view all candidates across all agencies
  • Cannot create Talkpush Owners

Agency Admin — Level 2

  • Manages users within their agency only
  • Can view all candidates submitted by their agency
  • Can upload new candidates
  • Cannot see other agencies' candidates

Agency User — Level 3

  • Most restricted role
  • Can only view and manage their own candidates
  • Can upload new candidates
  • Cannot manage any users
Role Escalation Prevention Users cannot assign roles higher than their own. A Company Admin cannot create a Talkpush Owner. An Agency Admin cannot create a Company Admin.

7. The Candidate Submission Flow (End-to-End)

This section describes the complete flow from the moment you click "Submit" until the candidate appears in both the Portal and Talkpush CRM.

Step-by-Step Flow

1

Fill Out the Submission Form

Enter the candidate's First Name, Last Name, Email, Phone, select a Campaign, and optionally attach a Resume (PDF or DOCX, max 10MB).

2

Validation

The system checks that all required fields are filled in, the selected campaign is active, and your agency is assigned to that campaign.

3

Duplicate Check

The system checks whether a candidate with the same email or phone number has already been submitted in this country. Based on the result, the candidate is assigned one of three tags: Added, Previously Submitted, or Duplicate. See What Each Tag Means below for details.

4

Resume Analysis (if attached)

If you uploaded a resume, the system reads the file and generates three AI-powered summaries: Professional Summary, Education, and Experience. These appear on the candidate's detail page.

5

Candidate Submitted to Talkpush CRM

The Portal attempts to create the candidate in the Talkpush CRM as a new application. If a resume was attached, it is also uploaded to the CRM as a document. If the CRM rejects the submission (for example, because the CRM does not allow duplicates for that client), you will see a warning — but the candidate remains saved in the Portal. See the CRM rejection note below.

6

Confirmation

You see a success message confirming the candidate was created. The tag (Added, Previously Submitted, or Duplicate) is displayed so you know the candidate's status.

Important: Regardless of the tag, the Portal always attempts to create the candidate in the Talkpush CRM as a new application. The tag is informational — it tells you whether this person was already known in the system, but it does not block the submission.
What if the CRM does not accept the candidate?
Some Talkpush CRM configurations do not allow duplicate candidates. If the CRM rejects the submission for any reason (including duplicates), the following happens:
  • The candidate is still saved in the Portal — no data is lost
  • You will see a success message with a warning: "Candidate created successfully, but failed to add to Talkpush CRM"
  • The candidate will not appear in the CRM — it only exists in the Portal
  • The CRM-specific error is shown in the warning so you can understand what happened
  • Contact your administrator if you see this warning repeatedly for the same country

What Each Tag Means

TagWhen It AppearsWhat Happens
Added This is the first time this candidate has been submitted in this country, OR any previous submissions have expired (outside the ownership window), OR the previous submission was withdrawn. Candidate is created in the Portal and in Talkpush CRM. Resume and AI summaries are processed. This is a brand-new referral.
Previously Submitted A candidate with the same email or phone was already submitted by your same agency within the ownership window (typically 24 months). Candidate is still created in the Portal and in Talkpush CRM as a new application. The tag lets you know your agency has submitted this person before. Resume and AI summaries are processed normally.
Duplicate A candidate with the same email or phone was already submitted by a different agency within the ownership window (typically 24 months). Candidate is still created in the Portal and in Talkpush CRM as a new application. The tag alerts you that another agency has already referred this person. Resume and AI summaries are processed normally.

Key Rules for Duplicate Detection

  • Detection is country-scoped — a candidate submitted in India does not affect a submission in the Philippines
  • Both email and phone are checked (if either matches, it counts)
  • Withdrawn candidates are excluded — if a previous submission was withdrawn, the new submission is tagged as Added
  • The ownership window (default 24 months) is configurable per country by your administrator. To change it, go to Countries → Edit Country → Ownership Window (see Section 13).

Example Scenarios

ScenarioTime Since Last SubmissionTag Result
Brand-new candidate, never submitted before—Added
Your agency submitted this person 2 months ago2 monthsPreviously Submitted
A different agency submitted this person 2 months ago2 monthsDuplicate
A different agency submitted this person 26 months ago (window expired)26 monthsAdded

20. Candidate Tagging System (Duplicate Detection & Ownership)

The tagging system automatically categorizes each submission to help track duplicate referrals. For a full explanation of how tags work and example scenarios, see Section 7: The Candidate Submission Flow.

Tag Types

TagMeaning
AddedNew candidate — first submission, ownership expired, or previous submission withdrawn
Previously SubmittedYour same agency already submitted this person within the ownership window
DuplicateA different agency already submitted this person within the ownership window
WithdrawnCandidate was withdrawn from the pipeline

Ownership Window

The ownership window is the time period (default 24 months) during which a candidate "belongs to" the agency that first submitted them. After this window expires, any new submission is treated as Added. The window can be configured per country by your administrator.

Key Points: Detection is country-scoped. Both email and phone are checked. Withdrawn candidates are excluded. All candidates are created in the CRM regardless of tag — the tag is informational only.

21. Talkpush CRM Integration

How the Portal Connects to Talkpush

Each country in the Portal is linked to a specific Talkpush CRM instance. This means candidates submitted for campaigns in different countries go to the correct CRM automatically.

What Gets Synced

DataDirectionWhen
New candidatePortal → CRMWhen a candidate is submitted
Resume filePortal → CRMWhen a candidate is submitted with a resume
AI resume summariesPortal → CRMAfter resume is analyzed
CommentsPortal → CRMWhen a comment is added on a candidate
Withdrawal statusPortal → CRMWhen a candidate is withdrawn
Pipeline stage (folder)CRM → PortalWhen viewing a candidate's detail page
Recruiter's notesCRM → PortalWhen viewing a candidate's detail page

What Happens if the CRM Rejects or is Unavailable

  • The candidate is always saved in the Portal first — no data is lost regardless of what the CRM does
  • If the CRM rejects the candidate (e.g., the CRM does not allow duplicates for that client), you will see a warning message: "Candidate created successfully, but failed to add to Talkpush CRM"
  • If the CRM is temporarily unavailable, the same warning appears — the candidate exists in the Portal but not in the CRM
  • Resume upload failures do not block candidate creation
  • Comment sync failures do not prevent the comment from being saved in the Portal
  • If you see CRM warnings consistently, contact your administrator — the CRM settings for that country may need to be reviewed

24. Cascade Enable/Disable System

The Agency Portal implements a cascade enable/disable pattern that maintains data integrity across the hierarchy.

Country disabled
└— All agencies in country → disabled
└— All users in those agencies → disabled

Agency disabled
└— All users in agency → disabled

What Disabled Means

Entity DisabledEffect
CountryAll agencies + all users disabled
AgencyAll users disabled
UserOnly that user disabled
Important: Disabled users cannot log in. Candidates remain visible regardless of entity status. Historical data is preserved — nothing is deleted. The system always verifies that the user, their agency, and their country are all active before granting access.

26. Frequently Asked Questions

Candidates

Why was my candidate tagged as "Duplicate"?

Another agency submitted a candidate with the same email or phone number within the ownership window (usually 24 months).

Can I change a candidate's tag?

Tags are automatically determined and cannot be manually changed.

What happens when I withdraw a candidate?

The candidate is marked as withdrawn in both the Portal and Talkpush CRM. They are excluded from future duplicate detection.

Can I see candidates submitted by other recruiters in my agency?

Only if you are an Agency Admin (via the Agency Pool). Regular Agency Users can only see their own candidates.

Why can't I see the "Stage" column?

The stage column visibility is a per-country setting controlled by your administrator. If you cannot see it, your administrator may have disabled stage visibility for your country.

Resumes

What file types can I upload?

PDF (.pdf) and Word documents (.docx). Maximum file size is 10MB.

Is the resume sent to Talkpush?

Yes, the original resume file is uploaded to Talkpush CRM as a document attachment.

Users & Access

My activation link expired. What do I do?

Ask your administrator to click "Resend Invitation" on your user profile.

Why can't I log in?

Possible reasons: (1) Account deactivated, (2) Agency disabled, (3) Country disabled, (4) Incorrect credentials, (5) Account not yet activated.
